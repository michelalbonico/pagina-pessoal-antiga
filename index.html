My carrier has always been driven by curiosity, courage, and a bit of madness. Nowadays, I'm an adjunct (between assistant and associated) professor at the Federal University of Technology - Paraná (UTFPR), Brazil. But the story is longer than that... I started my carrier as an office boy and while in the office I had the opportunity to work with what was love at first sight, computers. Computers need to be connected, right!? - at least I am not that old - We had some Linux servers there, so I met my second love (yeah, I have human loves as well), open-source software and Linux. I have been Linux user (mostly exclusively) since 2003. Linux also drove me through infrastructure/internet provider, where I worked as a network/content manager. Well, then, I started my academic carrier. I had a great opportunity to work as a bachelor lecturer since 2008 when I only held a specialization degree (in Brazil, it is a kind of professional degree in a specific subarea). During this, I did my Master degree at UFPR (Curitiba, Brazil), which led to a Junior/Assistant Professor position at UTFPR (Francisco Beltrão, Brazil), where I hold a tenured position now. During my first years at UTFPR, I did a Ph.D. (on leave) in Software Engineering/Distributed Systems at the IMT Atlantique (Nantes, France). Awesome life and professional experience. Being at UTFPR also keeps my learning horizon wide. In 2020 I started as a postdoctoral researcher in the S2 Group, at VU Amsterdam (Amsterdam, the Netherlands), mostly online given the COVID-19 pandemic. Then, in 2021, I had the privilege to work on-site for one month with that wonderful team. Another super great period of learning and friendship. OK, I forgot to say that when it comes to research, my interest lays in empirical software engineering, software engineering practice, and distributed software architectures... not limited to that. I am always open to collaboration and new adventures...

Teaching

Software Engineering

We're modernizing this. The plan is to split it into multiple credits. For now, I'm teaching generic SE, with a focus on software construction, validation and documentation

Introduction to Programming Languages (Not Always)

I teach first period students how and why to use programming languages. Usually, I mix Scratch (logic), C (Hello, world!), and Arduino (sensitive case study).

Distributed Applications

Basics of distributed systems, and case study projects. Java (moving to Python for ditactical reasons) is the main language here (threads, synchronization, remote calls, etc.), with some work on Spring framework. The course also presents some advanced aspects, such as frontend and backend separations, application framework (Spring), REST, RPC and messaging protocols (MQTT, WebSockets, etc.). At the end of the course, the students must deliver their own distributed application.
